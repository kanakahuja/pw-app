Page 1 of 3 Core Concepts
Refraction & Snell's Law
What is Refraction? When light travels from one transparent medium to another, it changes speed and bends at the interface. This bending is refraction.
n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂   (Snell's Law)
n = refractive index  ·  θ = angle from normal (not surface!)
"Always measure angles from the normal, never from the surface — this is the most common board exam mistake."— Sir at 08:45 in lecture
Refraction at Air→Glass interface
Page 2 of 3 Refractive Index
Refractive Index & Speed of Light
Absolute Refractive Index (μ): Ratio of speed of light in vacuum to speed in that medium.
μ = c / v   (c = 3×10⁸ m/s)
Air ≈ 1.0  ·  Water = 1.33  ·  Glass = 1.5  ·  Diamond = 2.42
Higher refractive index = denser medium = light slows more = bends more toward normal.
"Diamond sparkles because its refractive index is so high — light undergoes total internal reflection inside it."— Sir at 31:20 in lecture
Page 3 of 3 Board Practice
Key Numericals & Exam Tips
Numerical 1: Air(n=1)→Glass(n=1.5), θ₁=30°. Find θ₂.
sin θ₂ = (1×sin30°)/1.5 = 0.333 → θ₂ ≈ 19.5°
Numerical 2: Speed in medium = 2×10⁸ m/s. Find μ.
μ = 3×10⁸/2×10⁸ = 1.5
"Why does straw look bent?" → Refraction at water-air interface bends light, creating an illusion. Write this exact reason in boards.
Total Internal Reflection: only when going denser→rarer AND angle > critical angle.
Most asked (boards): Definition of μ (2M) · Snell's Law numerical (3M) · Objects in water appear raised (2M)
